Void
Architecture 11A: Introduction to Visual Representation
Professor: Darrell Fields

This project took a given 2D precedent, The Musician by Georges Braques, and tasked us with translating the spatial qualities of the composition into a 3D figure to study phenomenal transparency and various methods of representation.

The first portion of studies were done on bristol paper, where the formal and spatial qualities of the precedent piece were investigated and observed.

Those qualities then were translated from the two-dimensional to the three dimensional in the form of a chipboard model. This model was used to further study spatial qualities and began development of three intentional spaces.

The three spaces were finalized in an original axonometric composition whose formal and spatial qualities called back to the precedent piece. Along with that, a cut-away composition revealed those three spaces and the process diagram showed how the axon was created through plan and section cuts.
