A Building With Parts
Architecture 11B: Introduction to Design
Professor: Andrew Atwood

The first part of this project took a given number, 6, and precedent building, Ordos House by MOS to be broken down into a conceptual massing model that could be slotted together with no other adhesives.

I grouped the 12 individual roofs of the building into pairs to get 6 pieces. The connecting rods for those pieces are drawn from the floorplan as corridors and circulation within the house.

In the next stage of the assignment, using 4 of the pieces from the conceptual massing model, the word aligned was given to influence the design of a new building.

Every subsequent class, somewhat arbitrarily, new elements were imposed on the building: a site, 6 rooms, 8 doors, 2 stories, 3 stairs, 11 columns, 12 windows, 1 bathroom, 13 benches, and 2 entrances. Each of these additions had caveats and challenges that forced a reconsideration of the assigned word, and how to adapt these elements within the overall scheme.
