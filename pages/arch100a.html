Intersection
Architecture 100A: Architectural Design
Professor: David Jaehning

This project is a new community center situated on 50’ x 100’ site adjacent to the North Berkeley Bart station. The site is intersected by a 20’ wide path that cuts horizontally across it.

The moment where the path splits the site was extruded and became the main staircase and circulation for the building. From the exterior, circulation space is distinct from program space. The circulation is open air and shaded with a semi-transparent mesh while the program spaces are concrete and sealed.

To the north of the split is an art gallery, community storage area, and open roof. To the south is an office area, cafe, and study space.
